The Game of Anvil

ANVIL is a fantasy roleplaying game where you will become an elite dwarven taskforce, dropping in to hotzones to accomplish missions for the Queen. Within each mission you'll have to succesfully accomplish objectives while managing how covert your squad acts.

There is no wrong way to move through a mission-whether you are skulking through each room expertly dispatching foes without being seen or blasting your way through walls and sending deepbeasts back into the abyss what matters is accomplishing the objective. Sure, the Queen's Hand might not be happy with you lighting off a pile of powder kegs but that's a problem for later.

Upon succesful return from a mission the job isn't over. You'll navigate the complicated web of agendas throughout the realm to decide who to deal with and when, but not before crushing a pint of the finest ale with your squad, trauma bonding isn't the only way to build relationships.

ANVIL is about managing your impact, execution and the GM and world's ability to respond to your actions. Every dwarf has a plan until their fuse is cut short. Use any method you can to drop 'em like stones.

The Core Loop - How to Play

A condensed reference for how a full campaign session flows. Use it as a checklist during play. Full rules for every step are in their respective sections.
Phase 01 Prep - At the Overhold

Each dwarf takes up to 2 Overhold Actions. Actions cost Embers from the squad's shared bank. No dwarf may take the same action twice in one downtime.

Slag Off     1 Ember   Decompress. Prevent or redirect a Scar.
Train        2 Ember   Level one skill.
Forge        3 Ember   Upgrade Runeaxe or Explosives Kit.
Scout        1-3 Ember Reveal pieces of mission intel.
Pull Strings 3 Ember   Shift faction relations or Rep by 1.
Interrogate  1 Ember   Extract intel from a prisoner or artifact.
Reinforce    2 Ember   Add a single-use mission asset.
Bond         1 Ember   Advance a Bond with one squadmate.
Memorialize  -         Honor the dead. Grants +1 Rep.
Mission Briefing

The Hand assigns the mission. Players receive Briefing Intel: rough layout, primary and secondary objectives, Ember payouts, known faction presence, and drop points. Scout intel fills in additional keyed rooms if the action was taken.

Loadout

Players fill their 4 item slots from the prep stash. All operatives begin with a Runeaxe (CMP d6) and Explosives Kit (IMP d6). Stash access depends on Squad Reputation. Gear costs Embers at purchase.

Phase 02 Drop In

The squad chooses their drop point. Entrances are defined in the mission sheet with characteristics: guarded, trapped, structural, gear-gated. They arrive by Strigwraith or on foot.

GM Sets the Table
  • GM starts their Aftermath pool at the number of players.
  • Heat starts at 0.
  • Beat count starts at 0.
  • Hammers are prepped and ready.
Phase 03 The Mission

Each dwarf moves and takes an action. When the outcome is in question, throw stones.

Roll CMP and IMP. Assign one die as FUSE, one as BLAST.
FUSE - Execution, Speed, Precision
1-2 Clean, efficient, fast
3-4 Standard, stable
5-6 Messy, slow, complicated

Lower is better. Used against NPC Posture in social rolls.

BLAST - Impact, Noise, Force
1-3 Subtle, controlled
4-6 Loud, disruptive - raises Heat +1

Checked against Temper to hit. Marks NPC Trust on 4-6.

Explosive Actions [E]

FUSE is detonation time. BLAST is how many Explosion Dice to roll. Heat rises by the full BLAST die value, not just +1.

Smother

Both assigned dice show 1 on a standard action - reduce Heat by 3.

Stack Up

Multiple dwarves acting together. Pool all rolled dice, assign one FUSE and one BLAST from the pool. If two or more 6s appear across the full pool, Heat +1 plus 1 per participating dwarf. Cannot trigger Smother unless a rule says otherwise.

Beats & Aftermath

Mark 1 Beat after all players act. Every 3 Beats: GM gains 2 Aftermath dice. The GM may spend Aftermath at any point after a player turn to throw stones for enemies, traps, or the environment (minimum 2 dice per throw).

Hit Resolution
BLAST > Temper   Clean hit. Fills all 3 marks.
BLAST = Temper   Maim. Fills 1 mark.
BLAST < Temper   Botch. No effect.

Hits land on gear first or skills, then GRIT. When no gear or skills remain, roll d4 for both CMP and IMP. GRIT is last - 3 GRIT per dwarf. Marking the final GRIT means the dwarf is going down. One last action, then they're gone.

Phase 04 Extract & Downtime
Debrief Questions
  • Did Heat hit any Hammer thresholds?
  • Primary objective complete?
  • Secondary objectives complete?
  • Was ANVIL exposed? Were civilians or factions involved?
  • What rumors spread?
Ember Payout & Rep Shift
Cold extraction       -2 Rep marks
Warm extraction       -1 Rep mark
Hot extraction         no change
White Hot extraction   +1 Rep mark
Shatterpoint           +2 Rep marks
Recovery

All GRIT restored. Surviving dwarves roll for Scars. Funerals are held. Retirements are named. Then back to Phase 1.

Throwing Stones

Composure & Impact

Every dwarf has two dice: Composure (CMP) and Impact (IMP). The base die for both is a d3. All ANVIL operatives are assigned a Runeaxe and an Explosives Kit that raises each die to d6.

Rolling for the results of an action is called throwing stones. When you throw stones you roll both dice and assign one as FUSE and one as BLAST.

FUSE is about how. BLAST is about what.
You want to… Assigned die logic
Move quietly FUSE (lower = you get further)
Hit hard through armour BLAST (higher = more force)
Execute cleanly in combat FUSE (lower = clean and swift)
Make noise as a distraction BLAST (higher = louder)
Charm or de-escalate an NPC FUSE (lower beats Posture)
Blow something up right now FUSE (lower = immediate)
Cause maximum damage BLAST (higher = more dice)
Keep Heat down BLAST (lower = no Heat raise)

There is no "correct" assignment. Every roll is a trade-off between how cleanly something happens and how much chaos it creates. That tension is ANVIL's core.

Temper notation: T2 BLAST 2+ to handle. T2[++] two Explosive hits to destroy. T[++] cannot be handled by standard actions. An Explosive hit is always a clean hit.

Aftermath & Beats

Aftermath Pools

Aftermath dice are a pool of d6 used by the GM to react and let the world put pressure on players. The GM must spend a minimum of 2 aftermath dice to throw stones. All Aftermath dice reset between missions.

Generating Aftermath
  • Mission start: Gain Aftermath dice equal to number of players.
  • Every 3 Beats: Gain 2 Aftermath dice.
  • Any assigned 6s: Gain 1 Aftermath if a throw stones contains 6s.
  • Explosion Dice: Gain Aftermath dice equal to any Explosion Dice rolled.
Squad Aftermath

Some skills and situations may grant Squad Aftermath dice, kept separate from the GM's pool. Any dwarf may take 1 Squad Aftermath die before rolling to add it to their throw stones pool. Assign FUSE and BLAST as normal, discard the unused die.

Free Moves

The GM can make a free move at any time without needing Aftermath. Free moves are foreshadowing - the world around the squad. They never directly threaten or act against players.

Free Move Examples: A patrol completes its route and begins again. A light goes out or comes on. A door closes or locks. An enemy becomes suspicious but takes no action. The environment shifts: water rising, fire spreads, rubble settles.
Time and Beats

Mark 1 Beat after all players have had a turn. Gain 2 Aftermath dice every 3 Beats. Time within the world is not specifically tracked - the Heat gauge and Agendas pace the session.

Combat, Damage & Staying Alive

How Combat Works

Combat is not a separate phase. There is no initiative, no combat round, no turn order. Players act freely, the GM reacts with Aftermath. When something attacks, describe the action and throw stones. Low FUSE is a quick clean execution; high FUSE turns into a brawl.

If two entities wish to attack simultaneously, the lower FUSE hits first.

Temper in Combat

Every dwarf has a base Temper of T3. Skills and items can modify this.

Enemy Temper
Guard in leather T2
Guard in chainmail T3
Guard in blast resistant platemail T4[++]
Hulking deepbeast T5[+++]

Gear, Skills & GRIT

Hits land on gear or skills first, then GRIT. Players may choose what absorbs - the fiction should support it.

Gear & Skills

All gear and skills have 3 marks. When gear marks are full, it is unavailable for the rest of the mission. A dwarf with no gear rolls d3 for both CMP and IMP. Filled skill marks do not clear unless the Slag Off downtime action is taken.

GRIT

Every dwarf starts with 3 GRIT - their final resolve.

  • Clean hit to GRIT: marks all 3.
  • Maim to GRIT: marks 1.
Death

When a dwarf marks their final GRIT they are going down. They may take one last action - it must be plausible given their position and condition, and they may choose to reroll their throw stones. Then they are gone.

Enemies and traps can target skills or gear directly. A smart guard may attempt to ignite your satchel charges.

Social Interaction

Posture & Trust

Every NPC has a Posture on a scale of 1-6. If unsure, roll a d6. Every NPC also has 3 Trust marks. When all Trust marks are filled it is destroyed and the NPC turns Hostile.

Posture State
6 Hostile: will attack or raise alarm immediately
5 Suspicious: not buying it, one wrong move
4 Wary: cautious, watching
3 Neutral: not their problem yet
2 Friendly: willing to help or look the other way
1 Allied: may actively cover for the squad

Social Rolls

When a dwarf interacts with an NPC, throw stones.

  • FUSE under Posture: Posture down 1.
  • FUSE equal Posture: No change.
  • BLAST 4-6: Mark Trust.

NPC Posture and Trust does not reset between missions. Squads can reset Posture and Trust through using disguises or Overhold Actions.

Encumbrance

When carrying something the size of a dwarf or larger: roll an extra d6 when throwing stones, discard the lowest die before assigning FUSE and BLAST.

Two Dwarves Carrying

The penalty applies to both. Both roll stones with an extra d6, both discard the lowest die, then pool the dice together and assign.

Multiple Large Things

Roll an extra d6 for each item. Discard a number of lowest dice equal to how many items carried. Assign as normal.

Bonds

How Bonds Work

Dwarves build Bonds through surviving missions together and spending time at the Overhold. Track separately for each squad mate on your Op Sheet. Each Bond level requires 2 marks to advance. At campaign start, write down your squad and start each Bond with 1 mark filled. Bond levels are tracked identically on both dwarves' sheets.

Advancing Bonds
  • BOND downtime action: Establishes or advances a Bond. If the Bond does not exist yet, this establishes it at the first mark of level 1.
  • Surviving White Hot or Shatterpoint: Fill one mark on all active Bonds.
New Dwarves & Sync
  • When a new dwarf joins the squad, they start at Bond 0.
  • Bond levels are tracked identically on both dwarves' sheets. If one sheet says level 3 and the other says level 2, apply the lower level until both are updated.

Bond Levels

Bond Effect
1 Bonded. Your relationship exists. May Stack Up together.
2 Only 1 Encumbrance penalty when working together.
3 Aftermath from Stack Up actions is reduced by 1 (minimum 1).
4 Once per mission, when a bonded dwarf is targeted by a hit, you may become the target instead.
5 Stack Up actions between this pair may trigger Smother.

Gear & Stash

Item Tags

[E] Explosive: if struck, roll Explosion Dice.

[Q] Quiet: never raises Heat.

Runeaxe & Explosives Kit

These items aren't just weapons - they're tools and the foundation of any ANVIL operative's loadout. They have a high Ember cost to replace; take care of them.

  • Runeaxe: Main weapon and tool. Raises base CMP die to d6.
  • Explosives Kit: The only explosive gear that is not [~]. Raises base IMP die to d6. Infinite small charges. Additional and different explosives can be taken from the stash.

Both can be upgraded and modified with the Forge downtime action.

Overhold Stash

The Overhold maintains a stash of operational equipment available between missions. The GM maintains the stash list; it grows and shrinks with the campaign. Higher Rep tiers unlock deeper stash access. Oddities are never guaranteed.

  • Contents depend on Squad Reputation and Ember investment.
  • The stash is not a shop. Dwarves do not browse it.
  • ANVIL decides what is available and operatives requisition what they need.

Running Missions

Mission Structure

  1. The squad is assigned an objective by the Hand.
  2. They're given what intel exists.
  3. They drop in.
  4. They complete objectives.
  5. They extract.

Every mission has a map and a sheet. The sheet handles the macro structure. The map handles the room-level detail. Successful objectives and completed missions earn the squad Embers.

Room Key Format
04. FORGE HALL
A large hall filled with dark smoke and glowing furnaces.
4x Wardens (T3, sword), 1x Smithy (T2, hammer)
[SCALD]
Door to 05 is sealed. T[++]
- The wardens are forcing the smithy to work.

Drop Points & Extraction

The squad chooses their drop point from available entrances during Prep. Entrances are defined on the mission sheet with characteristics:

  • Guarded — patrols stationed at the entrance.
  • Structural — requires breaching to use.
  • Unique — requires specific gear or skills.
  • Some entrances are a mix of all three.

The squad can drop in via Strigwraiths (giant owls with silent flight) or move in on foot. The choice is theirs.

Extraction is improvised. The squad knows the exits but gets out however they can. A Hammer may close options, seal routes, or change what extraction looks like entirely. There is no guaranteed exit, but there is always a way out.

The Mission Sheet

The GM fills in the mission sheet during prep. Room-level detail lives on the keyed map.

  • Mission Name and Type: Name the mission and assign an objective type from the d8 table.
  • Objectives: Every mission has one primary objective and up to three secondary objectives. Ember payout is assigned per objective and revealed during briefing.
Ember Payout Guidance

Primary should always be worth more than a secondary. Adjust by mission weight — harder missions warrant higher payouts, shorter or lower-stakes missions can sit lower.

  • Primary: 3 Embers per dwarf. Completing only the primary covers basic downtime needs.
  • Each secondary: 2 Embers per dwarf. Two secondaries plus primary puts the squad in a comfortable position.
  • Full completion bonus: 1 Ember per dwarf. A windfall that rewards clean play.

Intel Tiers

Briefing Intel

Provided by the Hand. Official, possibly outdated, occasionally wrong. Gives: rough map layout, primary objective, known faction presence, recommended drop points.

Scout Intel

Firsthand reconnaissance gathered during downtime via the Scout action. Always reliable. Can fill in: patrol routes, enemy Temper values, entrance details, secondary objective locations.

The squad's pre-mission map is a partial key. Rooms with intel are filled in; everything else is dark until they move through it.

Ember Payout Guidance

Missions should pay out enough for each dwarf to take 2 Overhold Actions and get 2-3 gear per downtime without clearing every objective. Target: 5-7 Embers per dwarf per downtime.

Objective Payout
Primary objective 3 Embers per dwarf
Each secondary objective 2 Embers per dwarf
Full completion bonus 1 Ember per dwarf

Heat & Hammers

Hammers should radically change mission conditions. Every mission needs a Shatterpoint Hammer. Hammers should feel like consequences of the squad's impact, not arbitrary escalation.

Hammer Checklist
  • Does the Hammer change the physical space? (routes close, rooms flood, ceilings fall)
  • Does the Hammer change faction behavior? (reinforcements, elite units, full alert)
  • Does the Hammer affect at least one objective?
  • Is the Hammer something the players caused, not something passive?

Factions & Campaigns

Factions

Factions are the active forces in the world that ANVIL works against. They have goals, resources, and momentum of their own - they do not wait for the squad to come to them.

Each faction has an Agenda: a five-step plan they execute whether ANVIL intervenes or not. Each step has 2 marks to fill. When a faction reaches step 5 and completes their Agenda, a permanent campaign consequence locks in.

Agenda Steps
Step 1 Early movement. Quiet, deniable. Easily disrupted. Advances: 1-2
Step 2 Establishing. Resources and folks are in place. Advances: 1-3
Step 3 Entrenched. Disruption requires a focused mission. Advances: 1-4
Step 4 Operational. Bold, exposed, damage accumulating. Advances: 1-5
Step 5 Complete and irreversible. Campaign consequence locked in.

Disrupting Agendas

Mission Result Agenda Effect
Primary objective completed Agenda does not advance, no roll.
Primary failed, secondary completed GM rolls, +1 per secondary completed.
Mission failed entirely Agenda advances automatically.
All objectives completed Roll back the Agenda one mark.
Faction Moves (Between Sessions)

Each active faction makes one Move per session. It is not rolled - it is narrated at the start of the next session. Moves scale with Agenda step; a faction at step 4 does not care who notices.

Move Examples by Step
Step 1–2 Place an informant near a location the squad frequents. Begin quietly acquiring resources, personnel, or territory. Make contact with a potential ally faction. Establish a new site or forward position.
Step 3 Fortify a key location the squad will need to enter. Begin gathering intelligence specifically on ANVIL's methods. Move a high-value asset to a more protected position. Strike at a peripheral ANVIL resource.
Step 4–5 Place an elite operative or counter-squad in an upcoming mission. Issue a bounty on a specific dwarf by name. Strike directly at the Overhold: a burned contact, a compromised safe house, a threat delivered to the Hand. Forge an alliance with another faction, combining assets for one mission.

A faction at step 4 that successfully targets the Overhold marks Overhold Strain.

Overhold Strain

The Overhold has 3 Strain marks. Faction Moves that target ANVIL mark one Strain.

1 Strain The Hand is nervous. No mechanical effect.
2 Strain One Overhold Action becomes unavailable until Strain clears.
3 Strain Two Overhold Actions unavailable. Rep advancement frozen.
Clearing Strain
  • A successful Pull Strings action clears 1 mark.
  • Extracting from a mission at Cold to Hot clears 1 mark.
  • Only one mark may clear per downtime.

Example Faction: The Hollow Choir

Name The Hollow Choir
Nature Deep-cult accelerationists
Starting Step 2-2 (operating for months before ANVIL notices)
Completion The Wide Gate - titan deepbeasts awoken.
Key Figure Sevran - ritual leader, deeptouched, true believer
Tone Patient, convinced, increasingly bold

Multiple Factions

Two or three active factions means the squad is constantly triaging. Every downtime they pick one faction to run against. The rest keep moving.

Factions at similar Agenda steps may conflict with each other. When two factions are operating in the same location or competing for the same resource, the GM can drop that conflict directly into a mission.

Factions do not coordinate with each other unless their Agendas align and the GM decides an alliance makes fictional sense. A faction Move at step 4–5 can establish that.

Campaign Prep - Building a Faction

When building a faction, define:

  • Name and nature: Who they are and what they want.
  • Agenda steps: What they are doing at each of the five steps.
  • Completion consequence: What the world looks like if step 5 finishes.
  • Starting step: Most factions begin at step 1 mark 1.
  • Key Figures: Any major NPCs of the faction.
  • Tone: How to roleplay faction members.

Running a Campaign

A campaign is the full arc of multiple Faction Agendas from first movement to resolution. There is no fixed mission count. The campaign ends when every active Agenda is either completed or dismantled - or when the consequences of completed Agendas make further ANVIL operations untenable.

Tight Single Faction
~7-10 missions
Two Factions in Tension
~10-15 missions
Three Factions
Long campaign. The squad will not stop all of them.
Between Sessions, Ask:
  • What is each faction doing right now?
  • What does the squad know about it?
  • What happens if they ignore it for one more mission?

Downtime & The Overhold

Between missions ANVIL ops return to the Overhold. A large brutalist structure on the northwest edge of Glimmerhold, low forge smoke hangs in the air in every room. The Queen employs ANVIL and her Hand runs it; the Glimmerhold council does not know it exists.

Downtime is not rest. It is recovery, politics, preparation, and grief. It is your job between the jobs.

Embers

Embers are ANVIL's downtime currency. After completing missions all Embers go into the squad's shared bank. Squads can bank Embers between missions. Embers spent on actions or gear are gone permanently.

Downtime Structure

Step 1 Debrief.

The squad reviews the mission with the Hand or their assigned handler.

Players and GM work through:

  • Did Heat reach any Hammer thresholds? Was the primary objective completed?
  • Were secondary objectives completed?
  • Was ANVIL exposed, or were civilians or factions involved?
  • What rumors spread as a result?

The GM narrates campaign consequences.

This phase resolves:

  • Ember payout.
  • Squad Reputation.
  • Sets context of future missions.

Step 2 Recover.

Resolve Scars, funerals, retirements, and decompression. All GRIT is fully restored.

Give this phase space.

A dwarf down to their last GRIT who made it out needs a moment. A squad that lost someone needs a funeral. Name the dead, describe the funeral anvil, let the silence sit.

  • All GRIT is fully recovered here.
  • Dwarves who retire here roll a new operative.
  • Any player who's dwarf died during the mission rolls a new operative here.
If a dwarf dies mid-mission: a player may roll up a new operative immediately.
  • The table decides how they join the mission in progress.
  • Whether captive, a rogue operative, or a side cell also on the mission, etc.
  • Make sure the party joins up and works together witht them immediately.

Step 3 Overhold Actions.

Each dwarf takes up to 2 actions.

No dwarf may take the same action twice.
  • No dwarf may take the same action twice.
  • Actions require Embers burned from the squad's bank.
  • The base cost applies at Rep tier 3.
  • Rep modifies all costs:
    • tier 1 adds +2
    • tier 2 adds +1
    • tier 4 subtracts -1
    • tier 5 subtracts -2
  • Minimum cost is always 1 Ember.
  • Actions marked with no cost never require Embers.

Step 4 Mission Prep.

The squad reviews the next mission briefing together. Players interact with:

  • Mission objectives and Ember payout
  • The prep stash and available gear
  • Loadout selection
  • Insertion plan and drop point
  • Extraction route

Gear in the prep stash is available to requisition. Some items may cost Embers. The squad decides together who carries what. When loadouts are set the mission begins.

Scars

Rolling for Scars

Scars are permanent. They cannot be removed or healed but they are not punishments, they are changes. Some may make certain things harder while some open up new ways to operate. Some are just there, part of who the dwarf is now.

  • A player may instead choose a relevant scar.
  • Write one sentence about how it happened and mark it on your Op sheet.
  • If a dwarf rolls a Scar they already have it deepens. The GM and player decide what that means.
  • Each dwarf has 4 Scar slots.
  • If playing a one-shot, roll a scar when the character is created.

After every mission each surviving dwarf rolls for Scars.

  • Roll a d6.
  • Add 1 extra d6 for every GRIT marked during the mission.
  • If any die shows a 1 or 2, gain 1 Scar.
  • Only ever gain 1 Scar per mission regardless of how many 1 or 2s.
  • When all 4 are full and a new Scar would be gained, permanently lose 1 GRIT instead.

3 4d6

When the mission ends, your hands don't stop moving for days. Other dwarves recognize it.

Overhold Actions

Slag Off - 1 Ember

Drink deep, talk loud, decompress.

The dwarf spends time in the Overhold's lower halls, drinking, fighting, and relaxing.

  • Clear all marks on a skill.
  • May generate a rumor. Ask the GM one question during briefing.

Train - 2 Ember

Evolve a skill.

The dwarf levels one skill of their choice. Choose one upgrade type and rewrite the skill line. Each upgrade type may only be taken once per skill. A skill can be leveled three times.

  • Extend — applies to the whole squad, not just you.
  • Deepen — triggers in one additional situation or context.
  • Sharpen — the mechanical effect becomes more decisive.
  • Linger — the effect lasts longer or carries into the next phase.
  • Bend — add one exception to a core rule, once per mission.
  • Survive — the skill gains one extra mark.

Forge - 3 Ember

Craft specialized gear.

The dwarf works with the Overhold's armourers to upgrade their Runeaxe or Explosives Kit. May also request a piece of specialized custom equipment - the GM and player define it together.

Scout - 1-3 Ember

Reveal mission intel.

The dwarf runs advance reconnaissance on a potential mission target. Reveals one piece of hidden mission intel per Ember: layout, Temper of key enemies, Hammer thresholds, faction presence, or extraction complications.

Pull Strings - 3 Ember

Manipulate Glimmerhold politics.

The dwarf works back channels inside Glimmerhold to shift faction relations, suppress rumors, or reposition ANVIL's political standing.

  • May adjust Squad Reputation by 1 mark.
  • May affect future mission assignments.
  • Clears 1 Overhold Strain mark.

Interrogate - 1 Ember

Extract intel from prisoners or artifacts.

The dwarf works on a mission asset: a captured enemy, a recovered document, a strange relic. Generates specific actionable intel or reveals lore about enemy factions.

Reinforce - 2 Ember

Add temporary mission assets.

The dwarf requisitions additional resources for the next mission: a support contact, a safe extraction point, a distraction, borrowed heavy equipment. These are single-use mission assets.

Bond - 1 Ember

Create or advance a squad link.

Two dwarves spend downtime together and establish or advance a Bond. Fill one mark on the Bond track. If the Bond does not exist yet, this establishes it at the first mark of level 1.

Memorialize - 0 Ember

Honor the dead and retired.

The dwarf formally adds an anvil to the Overhold's memorial chamber for a fallen or retired operative. Memorialized operatives permanently affect the Overhold in small ways: a name on a plaque, a runeaxe mounted above the forge, their methods taught to new recruits.

  • Grants +1 Rep.
  • May unlock a one-time benefit tied to what that dwarf was known for.

Rep Tier Effects

Reputation reflects how ANVIL's handlers, rivals and enemies perceive the squad.

  • Reputation is not a measure of skill, it is a measure of tactics.
  • A squad at Myth leaves absolutely nothing behind, no witnesses, no evidence, no name.
  • A squad at Legend gets the job done but the job echoes out.
