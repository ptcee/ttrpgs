# FACTIONS & CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

---

## Factions

Factions are the active forces in the world that ANVIL is working against. They have goals, resources, and momentum of their own. They do not wait for the squad to come to them.

Each faction has an **Agenda** — a five step plan they are executing whether ANVIL intervenes or not. Each step the squad does not disrupt, the faction moves closer to completing it. When a faction reaches step 5 and completes their Agenda, a permanent campaign consequence locks in. The world changes. It does not change back.

The GM defines each faction's Agenda during campaign prep. Factions do not need to be fully detailed upfront — their Agenda steps and Moves can be built session to session as the campaign develops.

---

## Faction Agendas

Each Agenda has five steps. The GM writes a short description of what the faction is doing at each step and how the world looks if that step completes unchecked.

```
Step 1   Early movement. Quiet, deniable. Easily disrupted.
Step 2   Establishing. Resources and people are in place.
Step 3   Entrenched. Disruption requires a focused mission.
Step 4   Operational. Bold, exposed, damage already accumulating.
Step 5   Complete. Irreversible. Campaign consequence locked in.
```

The squad can only directly engage one faction per mission. Other factions keep moving while they do.

---

## Agenda Advancement

If the squad does not run a mission against a faction during a downtime, the GM rolls a d6 after debrief. On a result meeting or beating the threshold, that faction's Agenda advances one step.

The threshold depends on the faction's current step. Higher steps mean more momentum — a faction that has been left alone long enough is harder to slow down.

```
Step 1   Advances on 5-6
Step 2   Advances on 4-6
Step 3   Advances on 3-6
Step 4   Advances on 2-6
```

Step 5 does not roll. If the squad does not run a mission to stop it, it completes.

---

## Disrupting Agendas

Running a mission against a faction can stall, hold, or roll back their Agenda depending on the outcome.

**Primary objective completed:** the faction's Agenda does not advance this downtime. No roll.

**Primary objective failed, secondary completed:** GM rolls as normal. On a success, the partial work holds the line — Agenda does not advance. On a failure, it does.

**Mission failed entirely:** Agenda advances automatically. No roll.

**Exceptional outcome** — objectives completed, extracted clean, significant faction asset destroyed: roll back the Agenda one step. The faction loses ground.

---

## Faction Moves

Between sessions each active faction makes one **Move** — a concrete action that reflects their current Agenda step and what happened last mission. The GM chooses the Move that makes the most fictional sense. It is not rolled. It is narrated at the start of the next session or woven into the mission briefing.

Moves scale with Agenda step. A faction at step 1 is cautious. A faction at step 4 does not care who notices.

### Step 1-2 Moves
- Place an informant or observer near a location the squad frequents
- Begin quietly acquiring resources, personnel, or territory
- Make contact with a potential ally faction
- Establish a new site or forward position

### Step 3 Moves
- Fortify a key location the squad will need to enter
- Begin gathering intelligence specifically on ANVIL's methods
- Move a high value asset to a more protected position
- Strike at a peripheral ANVIL resource — a contact, a cached supply drop

### Step 4-5 Moves
- Place an elite operative or counter-squad in an upcoming mission location
- Issue a bounty on a specific dwarf by name
- Strike directly at the Overhold — a burned contact, a compromised safe house, a threat delivered to the Hand
- Forge an alliance with another faction, combining assets for one mission

A faction at step 4 that successfully targets the Overhold marks **Overhold Strain**.

---

## Overhold Strain

The Overhold has three Strain marks. Faction Moves that successfully target ANVIL infrastructure, personnel, or political standing mark one Strain.

```
Strain [ ] [ ] [ ]
```

At one Strain the Hand is nervous. No mechanical effect but the briefings get shorter and the stash thins slightly.

At two Strain one Overhold Action becomes unavailable until Strain clears. The GM decides which one based on what the faction targeted — a burned contact makes Interrogate unavailable, a compromised safe house might pull Reinforce.

At three Strain the Overhold is compromised. Two Overhold Actions are unavailable. The Queen's Hand is pulling back and watching the squad closely. Rep advancement is frozen until Strain drops below three.

**Clearing Strain:** one mark clears per downtime in which the squad completes a successful Pull Strings action or extracts from a mission at Cold or Warm. Only one mark clears per downtime regardless of how many clean missions were run.

---

## Multiple Factions

Two or three active factions means the squad is constantly triaging. Every downtime they pick one faction to run against. The rest keep moving.

Factions at similar Agenda steps may conflict with each other. When two factions are operating in the same location or competing for the same resource, the GM can drop that conflict directly into a mission — two hostile forces, neither coordinating with the squad, both in the way. Navigating that without becoming the thing that unites them is the squad's problem.

Factions do not coordinate with each other unless their Agendas align and the GM decides an alliance makes fictional sense. A faction Move at step 4-5 can establish exactly that.

---

## Running a Campaign

A campaign is the full arc of one or more Faction Agendas from first movement to resolution. There is no fixed mission count. The campaign ends when every active Agenda is either completed or dismantled — or when the consequences of completed Agendas make further ANVIL operations untenable.

A tight single-faction campaign runs roughly 5-8 missions. Two factions in tension runs 8-12. Three factions is a long campaign and the squad will not stop all of them.

The Hand assigns missions based on what the squad knows and what the Overhold can confirm. Between sessions the GM asks: what is each faction doing right now, what does the squad know about it, and what happens if they ignore it for one more mission? The answers build the next briefing.

---

## Campaign Prep — Building a Faction

When building a faction, define:

**Name and nature.** Who they are and what they want. One sentence.

**Agenda steps.** What they are doing at each of the five steps. Write it as action, not description — what they are building, taking, removing, or completing.

**Completion consequence.** What the world looks like if step 5 finishes. This should be irreversible and campaign-relevant. It does not end the campaign but it changes what future missions look like.

**Starting step.** Most factions begin at step 1. A faction the squad has already encountered in a previous mission, or one that has been operating for some time, might start at step 2 or 3.

## Example Faction — The Hollow Choir

*A depth-cult who believe the creatures emerging from the craters are ascending gods. They want to accelerate the process. Their methods are ritual, sacrifice, and patience. They have been operating beneath Glimmerhold longer than the Overhold knows.*

### Agenda: The Ascension Path

**Step 1 — Quiet Expansion**
The Choir is recruiting from desperate communities, establishing cells in abandoned infrastructure across the plateau. Individual members are hard to identify. Their gatherings are small and deniable.

*Completion: Two new cells are operational. The Choir has a presence in four locations across the Glimmerhold plateau that ANVIL does not know about.*

**Step 2 — Ritual Infrastructure**
The Choir begins preparing sites for large-scale rituals. Supplies are moved, locations are consecrated, and the most devoted members — those with visible depth-exposure — are positioned as site leaders.

*Completion: Three ritual sites are active and staffed. The Choir can now run simultaneous operations across the plateau.*

**Step 3 — First Contact**
The Choir successfully completes a minor ritual at one of their sites, establishing a stable connection to something below the craters. It is not a summoning. It is a conversation. The entity on the other side is patient and very interested.

*Completion: Sevran and two other site leaders have made direct contact. They begin receiving guidance. Their tactics sharpen. Their devotion becomes something else.*

**Step 4 — The Vessels**
The Choir begins acquiring sacrifices — not killing them, offering them. Willing hosts. Coerced ones. The ritual sites are now capable of permanent transfers. Something from below begins to walk in the world wearing borrowed faces.

*Completion: Three depth-touched entities are operating in Glimmerhold in human form. The Choir knows who they are. No one else does.*

**Step 5 — The Wide Gate**
The Choir completes the Ascension ritual at every active site simultaneously. The craters widen. What comes through is not one creature — it is a migration. The Underhold begins.

*Completion consequence: The northern quarter of Glimmerhold is lost within a season. ANVIL is formally acknowledged to the council under emergency powers. The Queen's Hand is no longer running a secret operation — they are running a war. Every future mission is hotter, better defended, and operating against enemies that know ANVIL exists.*

---

### Hollow Choir Moves by Step

**Step 1-2**
- A Choir cell relocates before a mission, leaving the squad hitting an empty site
- A new recruit is placed in a location the squad has used as a contact point
- The Choir quietly acquires explosives and tools from the same markets ANVIL uses

**Step 3**
- Sevran personally oversees the fortification of the next ritual site
- A depth-touched Devoted is assigned to monitor Overhold-adjacent locations
- The Choir sends a warning to ANVIL through an intermediary — stop now or what comes next is the squad's fault

**Step 4-5**
- A depth-touched entity in human form attends a downtime location — the Overhold's adjacent tavern, a contact's usual meeting point
- The Choir issues a bounty on the operatives who disrupted the Ashford compound by name and description
- A Choir Move targets the Overhold directly: a handler is approached, a supply line is compromised, a safe house burns

### Hollow Choir at a Glance

```
Name            The Hollow Choir
Nature          Depth-cult accelerationists
Starting step   2 (they have been operating for months before ANVIL notices)
Completion      The Wide Gate. The Underhold begins two centuries early.
Key figure      Sevran — ritual leader, depth-touched, true believer
Tone            Patient, convinced, increasingly bold
```
