# ANVIL — BUILDING MISSIONS & COMPACT FORMAT

---

## BUILDING A MISSION FROM SCRATCH

Use this checklist in order. Each step informs the next. A complete mission needs about 30-45 minutes of prep if you know your fiction.

---

### 1. Establish the Premise

Answer these three questions before touching the sheet:

- **What does the Hand need done?** One sentence. This is your primary objective.
- **Where does it happen?** Name the location and define its nature in a phrase. A decommissioned forge hall. A river barge moving through hostile territory. A cult compound carved into a cliff face.
- **Why now?** What has changed or is about to change that makes this mission time-sensitive? This feeds your Hammers.

---

### 2. Set the Objectives

Pick an objective type from the d8 table or choose one that fits the premise. Then build outward.

**Primary objective:** one clear success condition. Failing it is a failed mission. Assign an Ember payout of 3-4 per dwarf depending on mission difficulty.

**Secondary objectives:** up to three. These complicate the primary without replacing it. Good secondaries create meaningful choices — completing them makes the primary harder, or they require going somewhere dangerous you wouldn't otherwise need to go. Assign 1-2 Embers each.

**Full completion bonus:** 1 Ember per dwarf. Only if all objectives are completed and extraction is clean.

> Secondary objectives should never be required to complete the primary. They are upside, not gating.

---

### 3. Define the Faction

Name the faction present. Define their behavior at each Heat stage in one sentence each. Cold behavior should feel like a normal working environment. Shatterpoint behavior should feel like the building is on fire and they started it.

Ask: what is this faction protecting, and what do they do when they realize someone is taking it?

Assign Temper values to standard enemies now. Keep it simple — most grunt-level enemies are T2 with leather, T3 with chainmail. Name any named NPCs and give them skills if they warrant it.

---

### 4. Build the Map

Draw the map before keying the rooms. Sketching the physical space first prevents rooms from feeling like a list of encounters rather than a place.

**Map structure checklist:**
- [ ] 6-12 rooms for a standard mission
- [ ] At least two possible entry points
- [ ] At least one chokepoint the squad will have to deal with
- [ ] At least one room with no enemies but meaningful contents
- [ ] The primary objective location is not the first room
- [ ] The map has loops — dead ends only if they contain something worth finding

Mark which rooms are [DARK], [UNSTABLE], or carry other environmental tags before writing entries. Environmental conditions shape how the squad moves through the space.

---

### 5. Key the Rooms

For each room write:

- One line of atmosphere in italics
- Enemies present with Temper and gear
- Any environmental mechanics specific to this room
- Connections to adjacent rooms
- Anything interactive, lootable, or readable

Keep entries tight. If a room entry runs longer than six lines something can be cut or moved to GM notes.

**Room entry format:**
```
## 04. FORGE HALL
*[DARK] The furnaces are cold but the air still smells of iron and old smoke.*

2x Wardens (T3, chainmail, hammer)
The furnace controls are on the far wall. Activating them floods the room with heat — all targets in the room take a Maim from fire at the end of each Beat until it is shut off again.
One Warden carries a key to room 07 on a chain.

Connects to: 03, 05, 07 (locked)
```

---

### 6. Write the Hammers

Every mission needs a Shatterpoint Hammer. Hot and White Hot Hammers are optional but recommended for anything longer than six rooms.

**Hammer checklist:**
- [ ] Does the Hammer change the physical space? (routes close, rooms flood, ceilings fall)
- [ ] Does the Hammer change faction behavior? (reinforcements, elite units, full alert)
- [ ] Does the Hammer affect at least one objective? (the target moves, the item is destroyed, the window closes)
- [ ] Is the Hammer something the players caused, not something that happens to them passively?

Hammers should feel like consequences of the squad's noise, not arbitrary escalation. A mission that reaches Hot because the squad blew three walls should have a Hammer that reflects the faction responding to three blown walls.

Write each Hammer as a concrete event: what happens, what changes, what the squad now has to deal with.

---

### 7. Set Intel Tiers

**Briefing Intel** (all squads receive): rough layout, faction presence, one or two known entry points, objective location in general terms. This intel is official, possibly outdated, and occasionally wrong in one specific detail.

**Scout Intel** (if Scout action was taken): patrol routes, specific enemy Temper values, exact objective location, one Hammer threshold revealed, secondary objective details. Write 4-6 bullet points the GM can hand over cleanly.

---

### 8. Define Entry and Extraction

**Entry points:** two minimum. Give each a characteristic — guarded, trapped, requires specific gear, structurally compromised, exposed to sightlines from another room.

**Extraction:** note whether any Hammer affects extraction options. Note if the primary objective complicates extraction (a fragile item, an unwilling target, an incapacitated dwarf). Leave the actual extraction improvised unless the fiction demands otherwise.

---

### 9. Set Ember Payouts and Check the Math

Use the payout guidance from the mission sheet section. At Rep tier 3, the squad should be able to cover 2 Overhold Actions and 2-3 stash items per dwarf from a mission where they complete the primary and one secondary.

Run the numbers: primary payout × 4 players, then add secondaries. Does it land in the right range? Adjust if a mission is significantly harder or shorter than standard.

---

### 10. Write the GM Notes

One paragraph. Cover:

- Pacing intent — where should the mission feel tense, where should it breathe?
- One NPC worth playing with conviction
- What happens if everything goes wrong
- Any campaign hooks this mission plants for later

---
