## GM QUICK DROP

You are tracking three numbers during a mission: **Heat**, the **Aftermath pool**, and **Beats**. Everything else is fiction.

**Heat** is the mission's tension. It starts at zero and only goes up. Players raise it by assigning high BLAST dice and by blowing things up. You raise it when you spend Aftermath. Once it crosses a threshold, a Hammer drops and the mission changes. You defined those Hammers during prep. At Shatterpoint — 20 or higher — the mission begins collapsing. It does not end. They can still finish. But the clock is gone and everything that could go wrong is now going wrong at once.

**The Aftermath pool** is your capacity to push back. You start with dice equal to the number of players. You gain 2 more every 3 Beats. Any assigned 6 from a player roll adds 1. The pool is the world breathing, reacting, acting. A starved pool means the squad is winning on pace. A full pool means something is about to hit. You spend a minimum of 2 dice to throw stones for any enemy, trap, or environmental threat. Free moves — a patrol turning, a door swinging shut, a light going out — cost nothing. Use them constantly. They are how the world feels alive before the Aftermath starts swinging.

**Beats** are your clock. One Beat passes after all players have taken a turn. A player can hold still or do nothing but a Beat still marks. Every 3 Beats you gain Aftermath. Keep a running tally on scratch paper. You do not need to announce Beats to players but you should feel them.

**What a session looks like:** Players roll into the mission with a plan. That plan starts degrading the moment they interact with anything. Your job is not to destroy the plan — it is to make the world respond to their choices honestly. A loud action has consequences. A clean action opens options. The squad should always feel like they have agency and they should always feel like the clock is running. When the pool is lean, use free moves to remind them the world has not stopped. When the pool is full, spend into it. Play the enemies smart but not infallible. Most guards are doing a job, not hunting professionals.

**Die steps.** When a mechanic steps a die up or down, it changes from one die size to the next on the chain: d4 — d6 — d8 — d10 — d12. Stepping CMP down means a dwarf is rolling a smaller die and producing lower FUSE results. Lower FUSE is generally better. Stepping TNT up makes explosive outcomes larger and wilder. Neither direction is strictly punishment or reward — the fiction decides which matters.

**When to call for a roll.** If the outcome is obvious, do not roll. A trained dwarf picking a simple lock in an empty room gets through. Roll when failure has consequences, when the world can push back, when the tension in the moment matters. The dice do not resolve scenes — they create them.

## GAMEPLAY LOOP

> *This is a condensed reference for how a full campaign session flows. Use it as a checklist during play. Full rules for every step are in their respective sections.*

---

### PHASE 1 — PREP (Before the Session)

**At the Overhold.** Each dwarf takes up to 2 Overhold Actions. Actions cost Embers from the squad's shared bank. No dwarf takes the same action twice in one downtime.

```
Slag Off     1 Ember   Decompress. Prevent or redirect a Scar.
Train        2 Ember   Level one skill.
Forge        3 Ember   Upgrade Runeaxe or Explosives Kit.
Scout        2 Ember   Reveal one piece of mission intel.
Pull Strings 2 Ember   Shift faction relations or Rep by 1 mark.
Interrogate  1 Ember   Extract intel from a prisoner or artifact.
Reinforce    2 Ember   Add a single-use mission asset.
Bond         1 Ember   Advance a Bond with one squadmate.
Recovery     —         Full GRIT restored. Scars resolved. Funerals.
Memorialize  —         Honor the dead. Grants +1 Rep.
```

**Mission briefing.** The Hand assigns the mission. Players receive Briefing Intel: rough layout, primary and secondary objectives, Ember payouts, known faction presence, and drop points. Scout intel fills in additional keyed rooms if the action was taken.

**Loadout.** Players fill their 5 item slots from the prep stash. All operatives begin with a Runeaxe (CMP d6) and Explosives Kit (TNT d6). Stash access depends on Squad Reputation. Gear costs Embers at purchase.

---

### PHASE 2 — DROP IN

**Insertion.** The squad chooses their drop point. Entrances are defined in the mission sheet with characteristics — guarded, trapped, structural, gear-gated. They arrive by Strigwraith or on foot.

**GM sets the table.** GM starts the Aftermath pool at the number of players. Heat starts at 0. Beat count starts at 0. Hammers are prepped and ready.

---

### PHASE 3 — THE MISSION

**Player turn.** Each dwarf moves and takes an action. When the outcome is in question, throw stones.

> Roll CMP and TNT. Assign one die as FUSE, one as BLAST.

**FUSE** — execution, speed, precision.
- 1–2: clean, efficient, fast
- 3–4: standard, stable
- 5–6: messy, slow, complicated
- Lower is better. Used against NPC Posture in social rolls.

**BLAST** — impact, noise, force.
- 1–3: subtle, controlled
- 4–6: loud, disruptive — raises Heat by 1
- Checked against Temper to hit. Marked against NPC Trust on 4–6.

**Explosive actions (gear marked [E]):** FUSE is detonation time. BLAST is how many Explosion dice to roll. Heat rises by the full value of the BLAST die, not just 1.

**Smother:** Both assigned dice show 1 on a standard action. Reduce Heat by 3.

**Stack Up:** Multiple dwarves act together. Pool all rolled dice, assign one FUSE and one BLAST from the pool. If two or more 6s appear in the full pool, raise Heat by 1 plus 1 per participating dwarf. Stack Ups cannot trigger Smother unless a skill says otherwise.

**Any assigned 6s?** GM gains 1 Aftermath die.

---

**After all players act — mark 1 Beat.**

Every 3 Beats: GM gains 2 Aftermath dice.

---

**GM Aftermath.** At any point after a player turn the GM may spend Aftermath to throw stones for enemies, traps, or the environment. Minimum 2 dice per throw. Assign one to FUSE and one to BLAST as normal. GM FUSE is effectiveness. GM BLAST is checked against player Temper and raises Heat on 4+.

Free moves cost nothing: a patrol turns, a door closes, an NPC gets suspicious. The moment something threatens a player it costs Aftermath.

---

**Hit resolution.** When BLAST is checked against Temper:

```
BLAST > Temper   Clean hit. Destroys item or wipes all 3 marks.
BLAST = Temper   Maim. Fills 1 mark.
BLAST < Temper   Botch. No effect.
```

Hits land on gear first, then skills, then GRIT. Players choose what absorbs. The fiction must support it.

When a dwarf has no gear or skills left, d4 for both CMP and TNT.

GRIT is last. 3 GRIT per dwarf. Maim marks 1. Clean hit to GRIT marks all 3 and the dwarf is going down. They take one last action before they go.

---

**Heat thresholds.**

```
Cold        0–5    No Hammer
Warm        6–10   No Hammer
Hot         11–15  Hammer if prepped
White Hot   16–19  Hammer if prepped
Shatterpoint 20+  Hammer always. Heat cannot be reduced.
```

Hammers change mission conditions. They are never just more enemies — they close routes, flip room states, escalate faction behavior, wake something below.

---

**Objectives met? Extract.** Extraction is improvised. There is no guaranteed safe exit, but there is always a way out. Hammers may seal options. Shatterpoint is a race.

---

### PHASE 4 — EXTRACT AND DOWNTIME

**Debrief.** GM and players review the mission together.

- Did Heat hit any Hammer thresholds?
- Primary objective complete?
- Secondary objectives complete?
- Was ANVIL exposed? Were civilians or factions involved?
- What rumors spread?

Ember payout is distributed per completed objective. Squad Reputation shifts based on extraction Heat.

```
Cold extraction       −2 Rep marks
Warm extraction       −1 Rep mark
Hot extraction         no change
White Hot extraction   +1 Rep mark
Shatterpoint           +2 Rep marks
```

**Recovery.** All GRIT restored. Surviving dwarves roll for Scars. Funerals are held. Retirements are named.

**Overhold Actions.** Back to Phase 1.

## QUICK REFERENCE

### FUSE vs. BLAST — what does each die do?

Assigning dice is the core decision of every roll. As a rule:

> **FUSE is about how. BLAST is about what.**

Assign FUSE to the die that should reflect your control, speed, and precision. Assign BLAST to the die that reflects your impact, noise, and force. A dwarf can want low FUSE and low BLAST — silent and precise — or low FUSE and high BLAST — fast and destructive. High FUSE is almost never what you want: it means the action was slow, messy, or complicated.

```
You want to...                      Assign the higher die to...
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Move quietly                        FUSE (lower = quieter)
Hit hard through armour             BLAST (higher = more force)
Execute cleanly in combat           FUSE (lower = cleaner)
Make noise as a distraction         BLAST (higher = louder)
Charm or de-escalate an NPC        FUSE (lower beats Posture)
Set a charge with a long delay     FUSE (higher = more time)
Blow something up right now        FUSE (lower = immediate)
Cause maximum damage               BLAST (higher = more dice)
Keep Heat down                     BLAST (lower = no Heat raise)
```

There is no "correct" assignment. Every roll is a trade-off between how cleanly something happens and how much chaos it creates. That tension is the game.

### Temper at a Glance

```
T2         BLAST 2+ to act. One explosive hit destroys it.
T3         BLAST 3+ to act. One explosive hit destroys it.
T4         BLAST 4+ to act. One explosive hit destroys it.
T[++]      Cannot be handled by standard actions. Two explosive hits to destroy.
T4[++]     BLAST 4+ to act. Two explosive hits to destroy.
T5[+++]    BLAST 5+ to act. Three explosive hits to destroy.
```

**Baseline Temper values**

```
Operative (base)          T3
Unarmoured person         T1
Leather armour            T2
Chainmail                 T3
Blast-resistant plate     T4[++]
Standard door or lock     T2
Reinforced door           T3[+]
Stone wall                T[+++]
Depth creature (large)    T5[+++]
```

### NPC Posture

```
6   Hostile    Attack or alarm immediately
5   Suspicious One wrong move
4   Wary       Cautious, watching
3   Neutral    Not their problem yet
2   Friendly   Will help or look away
1   Allied     May actively cover for the squad
```

**Social rolls:** FUSE below current Posture → Posture drops 1. BLAST 4–6 → marks 1 Trust. Three Trust marks → NPC turns Hostile, no reset without disguise or Pull Strings.

---

### Damage Track

```
Gear and Skills: 3 marks each
  Clean hit → all 3 marks wiped, item destroyed
  Maim      → 1 mark filled

GRIT: 3 marks
  Clean hit → all 3 marked, dwarf is down (one last action)
  Maim      → 1 mark filled

No gear or skills left → d4 for CMP and TNT
```

### Aftermath

```
Start of mission        Pool = number of players
Every 3 Beats           +2 dice
Any assigned 6          +1 die
Explosive action pool   all rolled dice added after detonation

Spend minimum 2 dice to throw stones for the world.
Free moves cost nothing.
```

---

### Bond Levels

```
1   Relationship exists. May Stack Up together.
2   One encumbrance penalty when carrying together.
3   Stack Up Aftermath reduced by 1 (min 1).
4   Once per mission, redirect a hit from them to you.
5   Stack Ups between this pair may trigger Smother.
```

Advance by: Bond downtime action (1 mark) or surviving a mission at 2 or less GRIT (1 mark on all active Bonds).

### Overhold Action Costs

Base costs apply at Rep tier 3. Tier 1 adds +2, tier 2 adds +1, tier 4 subtracts 1, tier 5 subtracts 2. Minimum cost always 1 Ember.

```
Slag Off      1 Ember
Train         2 Ember
Forge         3 Ember
Scout         2 Ember
Pull Strings  2 Ember
Interrogate   1 Ember

Bond          1 Ember

```
